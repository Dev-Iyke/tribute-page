This past February, I lost my Dad to cancer. He was 59. I know all too well how empty the world can seem when we lose one of its greats. Because sometimes, just sometimes, the world produces exemplary forces of light, and that is what they were. So when their light dims, it's as if the world goes dark. But it's only a flicker. Sure, they're farther away - but their warmth, radiance, and glow - still lights the way for the rest of us. To my Dad and to Steve - wherever you are - I see you flickering. You are still lighting up the world.
